### **Reporting in Infosec – Key Notes**

**Why Reporting Matters**  
- Soft skills are essential: translating technical findings into client-ready reports.  
- Reports must be understandable by **both technical and non-technical audiences**.

---

### **Core Report Sections**

1. **Executive Summary**  
   - High-level overview for executives.  
   - Focus: **critical/high-severity issues needing immediate action**.  
   - Include a **severity breakdown chart** (e.g., 2 Critical, 4 High, 3 Medium, 1 Low).

2. **Overview of Assessment**  
   - Describe **methodology & tools used** (e.g., Nessus, manual validation).  
   - Outline the **assessment process** during the testing window.

3. **Scope and Duration**  
   - List **authorized targets** (IPs, domains, systems).  
   - Specify **start and end dates** of testing.

4. **Vulnerabilities and Recommendations**  
   - Only include **verified findings** (after false positive removal).  
   - Group by **vulnerability type or severity**.  
   - **Per finding, include**:  
     - Vulnerability Name  
     - CVE ID  
     - CVSS Score  
     - Description  
     - Affected Systems  
     - Proof of Concept (PoC)  
     - Remediation Steps  
     - References (e.g., NVD, vendor advisories)

5. **Closing**  
   - Emphasize clarity, conciseness, and proper grammar.  
   - Ensure technical details are **reproducible** and **well-referenced**.  
   - Remember: **the report is the deliverable**—it’s how your work is judged.
